Course Format, Schedule, and Deadlines:
All MIT xPRO courses are delivered online for maximum flexibility. Once course content is released, it will be accessible at your convenience. You will not be required to log in at any specific time to view it. See this FAQ article for more details

Your course will become accessible only after its start date and time, and course content will start to be released.

The course will have a specific schedule set by its instructors, which will determine when Course Content is released (weekly, for most courses). All Coursework will have a specific due date and time, which is strictly enforced. Late submissions will not be accepted for course credit.

The course session will conclude on the scheduled End Date, at which point live aspects of the course, such as the discussion forums, will close. Your Courseware will contain more specific details about the course schedule, which you will be expected to read and understand.

Accessing Your Course:
Once your course session starts, you will be able to open its Courseware and start learning! Access your {{ site_name }} Dashboard , locate your course, and click its name to get started! See this MIT xPRO FAQ article for details

Certificate of Completion:
When you complete the course with a passing score, you will earn a digital Professional Certificate from MIT xPRO, validating your accomplishment and expertise in {{ enrollment.run.title }}. Certificates are typically issued about 3-5 business days after the End Date of the course session. See this FAQ article for details

Where to Seek Support:
Once your course session starts, you should familiarize yourself with its Courseware—especially the first module, which typically contains important information introducing you to the course, its structure, schedule, and deadlines, and any special instructions or considerations unique to this course.

Additionally, each MIT xPRO course offers in-course discussion forums, hich will allow you to both engage with your fellow learners and seek support from your dedicated Course Assistant (CA) Team. The CA Team actively monitors these forums to offer assistance and guidance to all learners.

Any questions you may have about in-course matters, such as course content, coursework, and course logistics, should be posted to the Discussion Forums for the CA Team. They are best able to provide support for these inquiries.

Some xPRO courses may also offer other channels to interact with the Course Team. Check within your Courseware for more information, once your session starts.

For more general support questions, or questions containing sensitive or personal information, reach out to the MIT xPRO Support Team for assistance! Click here to submit a support ticket and we'll be happy to help! You can also find a lot of useful information in MIT xPRO Help Center.
